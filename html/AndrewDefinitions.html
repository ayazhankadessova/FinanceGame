What is stocks?
Definition: Stocks is a security that gives stockholders a share of ownership of the issuing corporation/company.
- Stocks has no maturity date.
- A stockholder will own the stock forever provided they do not sell the stock.
- Units of stock are called “shares” which entitles the owner to a proportion of the coroporation’s assets and profits equal to how much they own.
- Dividends are paid to shareholders based on profit made by company. May or may not receive the dividends.
- Highest risk.

What is bonds?
Definition: Bonds is is a type of security under which the issuer owes the holder a debt, and is obliged to provide interest to the lender. Can be issued by government or corporations.
- Bond interest is the amount paid by the holder to lender, which can be paid every month or year (usually per year).
- Capital amount lent by lender will be repaid at maturity date.
- Second high risk

What is Fixed Deposit?
Definition: Fixed Deposit is a financial instrument usually provided by banks which provides investors a higher rate of interest than a regular savings account until the given maturity date.
- Investors will deposit a sum of money to the bank, and it is block for the specified deposit period.
- One of the oldest and safest among investment instruments provided by banks.
