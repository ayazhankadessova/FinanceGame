Step 2: Identifying Needs and Wants

Do you really need it, or do you just want it?

Identifying your current needs and wants is one of the most important things in your financial journey.



Definitions

What is Need?
Definition: A need is something necessary to live and function. Food, shelter, clothing.
What is Want?
Definition: A want is something that can improve your quality of life. Branded items, leisure etc.
What is budgeting?
A budget is a plan that expresses your financial goals and how you will allocate your limited resources to achieve them.
Expenses can be grouped into: Inflexible Expenses, Flexible Expenses.
Alternative definition of budgeting
A budget is an income statement (cash flow statement) that is based on forecasted cash flows for a future time period.

In Real Life

Wow, isn't it amazing how we can distinguish between our wants and needs? By definition, a need is something essential to our survival, like having a roof over our head, proper clothing, food, work tools, and medication. On the other hand, a want is something we desire but isn't essential, like going on vacation, dining out at restaurants, playing video games, having a gym membership, or buying designer shoes.

However, it's worth noting that what one person considers a want may be a need for another. For instance, Samantha, who lives close to a bus route, may not consider owning a car a necessity, while Sam, who lacks access to public transit and can't cycle to work, might view a car as a crucial requirement.

By being able to differentiate between our wants and needs and budget well, we can save money for emergencies, retirement, or for purchasing something that we genuinely desire. Isn't that fantastic?

How To Budget

Note down your income and expense.
Label them e.g., Japanese $ 60. OR MTR $7
After one month, evaluate them.
Can use the spending apps e.g. Spendee.
Look at the things you can reduce, etc Eating out.
Change to alternatives: Cook more often.
